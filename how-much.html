How much you can get

Your Canada Dental Benefit payments will vary based on your family income. Parents and guardians with children who meet the eligibility criteria may apply for this benefit.

On this page

  • Benefit amounts based on income
  • Estimate your benefit amount
  • Maximum number of payments

Benefit amounts based on income

The CRA determines your benefit amount based on your adjusted family net income (AFNI).

The set benefit amount for each eligible child will not change based on your dental costs. If your dental costs are more than $650, you may be eligible to apply for an additional payment in the next benefit period.

How shared custody benefit amounts are split

You will receive half of the dental benefit amount if you have shared custody of your child and currently split the Canada Child Benefit (CCB).

Both parents can apply for the dental benefit if they are eligible. The amount you receive would be 50% of the benefit amount based on your adjusted family income. This means you may receive a different amount from your child's other parent.

The dental benefit uses the same custody arrangement as the Canada Child Benefit (CCB) as of:

  • December 1, 2022 for first benefit period
  • July 1, 2023 for second benefit period

Your adjusted family net income

The CRA calculates your adjusted family net income based on your previous year's tax return. We use this number to determine your eligibility for certain benefits.

You do not need to calculate this number. You can use the tool below if you want to estimate your adjusted family net income and your benefit amount.

Full custody

The dental benefit uses the same custody arrangement as the Canada Child Benefit (CCB) as of:

  • December 1, 2022 for first benefit period
  • July 1, 2023 for second benefit period

If the child lives with you more than 60% of the time, you are considered to have full custody for the purposes of the CCB.

For example, the child lives with you during the week and with another individual every second weekend.

Shared custody

The dental benefit uses the same custody arrangement as the Canada Child Benefit (CCB) as of:

  • December 1, 2022 for first benefit period
  • July 1, 2023 for second benefit period

If the child lives with you at least 40% of the time or on an approximately equal basis with you and another individual at a different address, then both individuals are considered to have shared custody for the purposes of the CCB.

For example, the child lives with you one week and with another individual one week, or with you 4 days a week and with the other individual 3 days a week (between 40% and 60%).

Universal child care benefit

The UCCB was a taxable benefit paid for children under 18 years of age, available to all eligible individuals, regardless of their income. Payments ended as of July 2016, but lump-sum amounts continue to be paid for months prior to that date. The Canada Child Benefit (CCB), which is an income-tested, non-taxable benefit, replaced the UCCB in 2016.

Maximum number of payments

If you have more than one eligible child, you can apply for all your children at the same time or separately. You may apply for a maximum of 2 payments for this benefit for each eligible child.

If you have shared custody of your child and currently split the Canada Child Benefit (CCB), each parent may receive a maximum of 2 payments for that child. In this case, each parent may receive 50% of the benefit payment amount.
