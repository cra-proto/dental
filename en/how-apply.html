How to apply

Applications are open for the first benefit period. If you have more than one eligible child, you can apply for all your children at the same time or separately.

On this page

  • Current period
  • Apply online or by phone
  • When to expect your payment

Current period

If you are eligible, the current period you can apply for is:

October 1, 2022 to June 30, 2023

The last day you can apply is Friday, June 30, 2023 (11:59 pm ET).

If you are eligible for a second payment, you must apply again during the next period.

Next period: July 1, 2023 and June 30, 2024

This period is not yet open for application.

The earliest you can apply is Saturday, July 1, 2023.

During this period, applications will be open for:

  • dental care received between July 1, 2023 and June 30, 2024
  • the additional payment for either benefit period

Apply online or by phone

The fastest way to apply for the Canada Dental Benefit will be online through CRA My Account. Make sure you're ready to apply.

  1. Get your information ready

    To apply, you will need:

    • each eligible child's dental care provider's name, address and telephone number
    • expected date of your child's dental appointment
    • your employer's name, address, and telephone number (if applicable)
    • your spouse or common-law partner's employer's name, address, and telephone number (if applicable)

  2. Apply

    Read the privacy statement

    When you apply for the Canada Dental Benefit, your personal information is collected under the authority of the Dental Benefit Act, which is being jointly administered by Health Canada and the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA). Your personal information will be used for the purpose of (i) administering your application, and could be used for (ii) issuing a payment to you, (iii) verifying that you meet the program's eligibility requirements, and to (iv) collect any overpayments or erroneous payments that may have been issued to you. Failure to provide information will affect your consideration for benefits.

    Please note that to process your application we will need to access information in your tax file. Your information may also be disclosed to other federal, provincial, territorial or Indigenous peoples' government institutions to the extent authorized by law. Failure to provide this information will make it impossible for us to process your application.

    Under the Privacy Act, you have a right of protection, access to and correction of your personal information and to file a complaint with the Privacy Commissioner of Canada regarding our handling of your information. Personal information banks for this program are under development and will be available in Info Source.

    Online:

    Apply using your CRA My AccountRegister

    Hours of service for CRA My Account

    Access CRA My Account from My Service Canada Account

    You can sign in to My Service Canada Account to access CRA My Account.

    In your My Service Canada Account:

    1. Click on "Access CRA"
    2. Select "I agree" on the Registration and Authentication page

    This transfers you to CRA My Account.

    By phone:
    Contact the CRA to apply by phone or TTY

    To confirm your identity, you'll need your:

    • Social Insurance Number (SIN)
    • full name and date of birth
    • complete address
    • assessed tax return, notice of assessment or reassessment, other tax document, or be signed in to CRA My Account
    • personal details about the children in your care
    • information from previous benefit payments

    Expect longer wait times

    You may experience longer than usual wait times to speak to an agent due to high call volumes.

  3. Keep your dental care receipts

    Keep your receipts for 6 years in case the CRA needs to validate your eligibility. You do not need to call us if your child's dental appointment is rescheduled within the same benefit period.

    Provide additional information requested by the CRA

    We may send you a letter to request information. You must respond within 30 days or you may need to repay the benefit. We may call you or your dental care provider to confirm the information you provide.

    Documents you may need to provide

    To validate your eligibility, you may need to provide one of the following:

    • receipt for dental care services
    • paid invoice for dental care services
    • dental statement of account
    • letter from the dental care provider

    Your document must include:

    • name and address of your child
    • name and address of the dental care provider
    • date of the dental appointment
    • amount paid for the dental care service

When to expect your payment

If you're eligible for the Canada Dental Benefit, your payment amount will be based on your family net income and the number of children you are applying for.

Standard processing times

Direct deposit
Direct deposit takes up to 5 business days if you have it set up with the CRA.
By mail
A mailed cheque should arrive in about 10 business days.
