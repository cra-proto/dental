Why you may need to repay

If you made a mistake or your situation changed after you applied for the Canada Dental Benefit, you may no longer be eligible. You will have to return any amounts you were not eligible for.

If your child was eligible and their dental costs were less than you received, you do not need to return any unused amounts.

If you provide false information

If you are found to have misrepresented or concealed essential information to make a claim, you will be ineligible for the benefit amounts you received and cannot apply again. You will have to return any payments you received.

If you think you made an honest mistake, contact us.

How to repay

If you were not eligible for the Canada Dental Benefit, you must return the amount you received for each child who was not eligible.

  1. Find out how much you received

    To confirm your amounts received:

    • check your bank statements or cheques
    • check your payments under "Benefits and credits" in your CRA My Account
    • contact the CRA at 1-800-715-8836 (opens up phone application)1-800-715-8836

  2. Determine how much to repay

    The benefit paid a set amount for each child. You must return the amount you received for each child who was not eligible. The amount you received per child depended on your adjusted family net income.

    Benefit amounts per eligible child

    Adjusted family net income Full custody amount Shared custody amount
    less than $70,000 $650 $325
    $70,000 to $79,999 $390 $195
    $80,000 to $89,999 $260 $130

  3. Make a repayment

    You can make your repayment to the CRA online, by mail, or in person.

    For options, go to make a payment

    If you have the original cheque

    You should only return the original cheque if you need to repay the full amount. You may return it by mail to the address below.

    • Revenue Processing – Repayment of ???
      Sudbury Tax Centre
      1050 Notre Dame Avenue
      Sudbury ON P3A 0C3

    Be sure to include:

    1. The reason for returning the cheque ("not entitled" or "overpayment")
    2. Your Social Insurance Number (SIN)

    If you cannot pay in full

    The CRA does not charge interest on Canada Dental Benefit debts.

    If you need more time to repay

    Contact the CRA to help you arrange to pay your debt over time.

    If the CRA has already contacted you about your debt, get details for the collection of overpayments.

    If you do not repay

    If you are unable to pay your debt right now or unable to pay it at all, contact the CRA to discuss your options.

    The CRA may keep all or a portion of future payments, income tax refunds, or goods and services tax/harmonized sales tax (GST/HST) credits until your balance owing is repaid.

    This may also apply to other federal, provincial, and territorial programs that the CRA administers.

Your adjusted family net income

The CRA calculates your adjusted family net income based on your previous year's tax return. We use this number to determine your eligibility for certain benefits.

You do not need to calculate this number. You can use the estimator tool if you want to estimate your adjusted family net income and your benefit amount.

Full custody

The dental benefit uses the same custody arrangement as the Canada Child Benefit (CCB) as of:

  • December 1, 2022 for first benefit period
  • July 1, 2023 for second benefit period

You must have either full or shared custody to receive the CCB. If you have full custody for the CCB, you will be the only one receiving the CCB. If you are not eligible to receive the CCB for your child, you cannot apply for the Canada Dental Benefit.

If your CCB custody arrangement has changed, make sure you update your information with the CRA.

What counts as full custody for the CCB

If the child lives with you more than 60% of the time, you are considered to have full custody for the purposes of the CCB.

For example, the child lives with you during the week and with another individual every second weekend.

For details: CCB child custody arrangements

Shared custody

The dental benefit uses the same custody arrangement as the Canada Child Benefit (CCB) as of:

  • December 1, 2022 for first benefit period
  • July 1, 2023 for second benefit period

You must have either full or shared custody to receive the CCB. If you have shared custody for the CCB, both you and your child's other parent will be receiving the CCB. If you are not eligible to receive the CCB for your child, you cannot apply for the Canada Dental Benefit.

If your CCB custody arrangement has changed, make sure you update your information with the CRA.

What counts as shared custody for the CCB

If the child lives with you at least 40% of the time or on an approximately equal basis with you and another individual at a different address, then both individuals are considered to have shared custody for the purposes of the CCB.

For example, the child lives with you one week and with another individual one week, or with you 4 days a week and with the other individual 3 days a week (between 40% and 60%).

For details: CCB child custody arrangements
